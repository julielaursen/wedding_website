We are getting married!

The date is 4th of September '22 at Star Hill Ranch and we would like you to be a part of it. Centered along a historic main street and surrounded by panoramic Hill Country views, Star Hill is located just 25 minutes west of downtown Austin in the community of Bee Cave. Star Hill is currently home to 22 historic buildings that have been relocated from around Texas. All were originally constructed between 1850 and 1935.

How do I get there?

From the airport/downtown, take Hwy 71 West to Bee Caves, TX

What's the dress code?!

Ceremony 4PM - 5PM

Sunday Best
We ask that you wear something nice that looks good in photos! We'd prefer no logo shirts, crop tops or running shoes. Although our venue is both indoor/outdoor, Texas can be hot and humid well into the fall. Therefore, we don't recommend wool or heavy clothing. The venue includes many historic buildings from the 1800s and early 1900s. Therefore, vintage clothing, flapper dresses, and western wear would all fit the venue well.

Reception 7PM - 12AM

Dance Wear Encouraged
We'll be dancing the night away so we highly recommend either dancing shoes or cowboy boots. Ladies, feel free to bring an extra pair of shoes if you want to change from sandals/heels to dance shoes. We will have room to change if needed.

Are children allowed?
We respectfully wish to keep this an adult only wedding
What's your COVID-19 policy?
We require our guests to be vaccinated or have a doctor's note if unable to get vaccinated for medical reasons
Is the venue BYOB?
No! TABC has strict rules about this. Only bartenders can serve alcohol at this venue. As a bonus, the bartenders love to talk about the history of the venue.
Is there anything important to know about the venue?
This is very important! Our venue includes many wood buildings that are over 100 years old. Please refrain from any smoking anywhere near the venue itself. There will be a designated smoking area a short walking distance from the buildings. Also, please refrain from going inside any buildings that are not designated with a sign in front of them. Buildings not directly involved in the ceremony/reception are considered private. Also, all decorations and centerpieces are rentals from the venue and must be left at the end of the night.
How is parking?
There should be ample room for parking at the venue.
What activities are on the schedule for the wedding? We hope to be one of the most fun weddings you've attended. Please grab a latte from the coffee truck when you come in. Between cocktail hour and the afterparty, part of the schoolhouse building will have tables for board games. Main street will have cornhole and giant jenga. The venue itself is a great opportunity. Though we ask that guests do not photograph or video the wedding (we have professionals covering that), take as many pictures of yourself as you'd like in the saloon, biergarten, or by some of the historic vehicles. Ralph Fisher's photo animal company will be providing beer burros and armadillo racing. Armadillo racing will come with prizes! We will also have an axe-throwing booth! Late Night: Post send-off we will hav a dancing after-hours party with a DJ and catering by Burro's artisan grilled cheese. Expect a mix of East Coast Swing, West Coast Swing, Two Step and Lindy.

What's there to do in Bee Caves, TX?
Our venue is a 2 minute drive from the Hill Country Galleria, which includes multiple restaurants and breweries as well as lots of shopping, Whole Foods, and a Gold's gym. On Saturday, the Hill Country Galleria may throw concerts or have a farmers market. See their website (TBD) for details. Our venue is also very close to Dripping Springs, Hamilton Pool and lots of breweries and wineries. The venue is also a short drive from Lake Travis in Lakeway and the Ladybird Johnson Wildflower center in South Austin. We'd recommend the Oasis in Lakeway for music and margaritas with a view.
