FAQ

What's the dress code?!

Ceremony 4PM - 5PM Sunday Best

We ask that you wear something nice that looks good in photos! We'd prefer no logo shirts, crop tops, cargo shorts or running shoes. Please do not wear stilletto heels as there are many gravel paths. Although our venue is both indoor/outdoor, Texas can be hot and humid well into the fall. Therefore, we don't recommend wool or heavy clothing. Polo short sleeve shirts, sundresses, jumpsuits and cocktail dresses all work. The venue includes many historic buildings from the 1800s and early 1900s. Vintage clothing, flapper dresses, and western wear would all fit the venue well and are encouraged.

Reception 7PM - 12AM Dance Wear Encouraged

We'll be dancing the night away so we highly recommend either dancing shoes or cowboy boots. Ladies, feel free to bring an extra pair of shoes if you want to change from sandals/heels to dance shoes. We will have room to change if needed.

What's your COVID-19 policy?

We require our guests to be vaccinated or have a doctor's note if unable to get vaccinated for medical reasons. PLEASE BRING YOUR VACCINE CARD WITH YOU.

Are children allowed?

We respectfully wish to keep this an adult only wedding

Is the venue BYOB?

No! TABC has strict rules about this. Only bartenders can serve alcohol at this venue. As a bonus, the bartenders love to talk about the history of the venue.

OPEN BAR...?

OPEN BAR!

Do I get a plus one?

Maybe! If you're coming from out of state, then you can bring any one person that you'd like. In town guests, we ask that you please rsvp for just yourself or your domestic partner/spouse. Later in the year, we may open the RSVP up to more family members/boyfriends/girlfriends if you let us know in advance that you want to bring someone, but as of now we are trying to cap guest count.

What events are scheduled besides the ceremony and reception?

We will have a rehearsal dinner that is invite only. No other events are currently scheduled.

Are gifts required?

No, but we won't look a gift horse in the mouth. A thoughtful card or your favorite cooking recipe(no garlic) counts, too!

Is there anything important for me to know as a guest?

Let us know about changes! If you RSVP but later need to cancel, please inform the bride and groom as early as possible. Catering and seating is per head and advanced notice allows us to recoup those costs.

Is there anything important to know about the venue?

This is VERY IMPORTANT! Our venue includes many wood buildings that are over 100 years old. NO SMOKING AND NO VAPING anywhere near the venue itself. There will be a designated smoking area a short walking distance from the buildings. Also, please refrain from going inside any buildings that are not designated with a sign in front of them. Buildings not directly involved in the ceremony/reception are considered private. Also, all decorations and centerpieces are rentals from the venue and must be left at the end of the night as they are not favors but rentals.

How is parking?

So much parking.

What will the catering consist of?

Please let us know if you have special dietary needs we need to accomodate. Our catering is provided by Verde's mexican restaurant. We will also have grilled cheese and grilled peanut butter sandwiches for late night and a barista truck in the late afternoon/early evening.

What activities are on the schedule for the wedding?

We hope to be one of the most fun weddings you've attended. We will have tables open for board games. Ralph Fisher's photo animal company will be providing beer burros and armadillo racing. We will also have an axe-throwing booth! Late Night: Post send-off we will have a dancing after-hours party with a DJ.

Social Media/Photography

The venue itself is a great photo opportunity. Though we ask that guests do not photograph or video the ceremony (we have professionals covering that), take as many pictures of yourself as you'd like in front of the historic buildings and vehicles.

What hotels do you recommend?

  • The Sonesta Hotel is very close and has a shuttle.
  • Marriott Springhill Suites Lakeway
  • Hampton Inn Lakeway
  • La Quinta- Lakeway or SW Pkwy & Mopac
  • Holiday Inn Express Lakeway
  • Sunset RV Park

What's there to do in Bee Caves, TX?

Our venue is a 2 minute drive from the Hill Country Galleria, which includes multiple restaurants and breweries as well as lots of shopping, Whole Foods, and a Gold's gym. On Saturday, the Hill Country Galleria may throw concerts or have a farmers market. See their website for details. Our venue is also very close to Dripping Springs, Hamilton Pool and lots of breweries and wineries. The venue is also a short drive from Lake Travis in Lakeway and the Ladybird Johnson Ladybird Johnson Wildflower Center in South Austin. We'd recommend The Oasis in Lakeway for music and margaritas with a view.
